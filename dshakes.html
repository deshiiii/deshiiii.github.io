DOUGAL SHAKESPEARE

504/GWT
 
I am a researcher in the field of Music Information Retrievel. My research interests revolve around further understanding how users make use of so-called “affordances” (e.g. algorithmic recommendation, personal playlists ect.) on modern day music streaming platforms and furthermore, the effect these adoptions have upon consumption diversity. 

Currently I am at the end of finishing my PhD (EHESS, Centre March Bloch - Computational Social Science Team) entitled “Exploring Multi-affordance Dynamics on Music Streaming Platforms”. 

I work within the RECORDS - a research project funded through an ANR grant (2020-23) in collaboration with Deezer, Orange Labs and the CNRS.
